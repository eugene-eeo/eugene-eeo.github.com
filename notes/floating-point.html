floating point

Basically standard form but in base 2. Current standard for floating point representation is IEEE-754. All finite numbers can be written as:

$$ (-1)^s \times c \times b^q $$

where:

  • $ b $ – base
  • $ s $ – sign (0/1)
  • $ c $ – significand of precision $ p $, integer $ 0 \leq c \leq b^p - 1 $
  • $ q $ – exponent, (signed) integer $ 1 - \text{emax} \leq q + p - 1 \leq \text{emax} $

Explanation for choice of $ q $:

$$ \begin{aligned} (b^p - 1) \times b^{\text{emax} - (p-1)} &= b^{\text{emax} - 1} - b^{\text{emax} - (p-1)} \\ &= (b - b^{-p+1}) \times b^{\text{emax}} \end{aligned} $$

Also when $ p = 1 $, $ 1 - \text{emax} \leq q \leq \text{emax} $. N-bit floating point number, with precision of $ p $:
