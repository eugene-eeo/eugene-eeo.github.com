Graph Cartesian Product

The cartesian product of two graphs, \( G \,\square\, H \) is defined as the graph with vertex set \( V(G) \times V(H) \), and with edges such that \( (x,y) \) and \( (x',y') \) are adjacent if one of the following is true:

  • \( y = y' \), and \( xx' \in E(G) \)
  • \( x = x' \), and \( yy' \in E(H) \)

The graph \( C_4 \) (cycle on 4 nodes) can be expressed as the cartesian product of two 2-node paths, \( C_4 = P_2 \,\square\, P_2 \). The first diagram shows \( C_4 \,\square\, P_2 \) to get a ‘cube’; doing it again with \( P_2 \) gives a graph that looks like a hypercube.

There are some easy results one can derive for the \( \square \) operation. If we define equality as isomorphism, then the identity element is the graph \( P_1 \) with one vetex and no edges, since \( G \,\square\,P_1 = G \) for all \( G \). We also get commutativity \( G \,\square\, H = H \,\square\, G \), and associativity \( G \,\square\, (H \,\square\, F) = (G \,\square\, H) \,\square\, F \).
